About Automated Schedule Generator

Most colleges have a number of different courses, and each course has multiple subjects. With limited faculties, each faculty teaches more than one subject. Creating a timetable that schedules the faculty at provided time slots, ensuring no overlaps, and making the best use of all faculty subject demands is a complex task.

The Automated Schedule Generator utilizes a genetic algorithm for this purpose. It involves a timetable object comprising of classroom objects and the timetable for each classroom, along with a fitness score for the timetable. The fitness score indicates the number of clashes the timetable has with other schedules for different classes.

The Classroom object consists of week objects, which, in turn, contain days. Each day is divided into time slots, and each time slot is assigned to a specific subject, student group, and teacher.

The algorithm is designed to be flexible and easily extendable to add or remove various duties. Constraints are checked between two timetable objects, and if a clash is detected, the score is increased accordingly.

The Automated Schedule Generator is designed to help educational institutions efficiently manage their schedules, ensuring optimal utilization of faculty and resources while minimizing conflicts and clashes.
